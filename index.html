Description


The Sundarban is a region of mangroves formed as the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna rivers merge in the Bay of Bengal. The Sundarban Reserve Forest (SRF) is situated in Bangladesh and is the largest mangrove forest globally. It extends from Bangladesh's Khulna division's Baleswar River to India's West Bengal state's Hooghly River.By promoting responsible and sustainable tourism practices, the Sundarbans can continue to be a world-renowned eco-tourism destination while also safeguarding its unique natural and cultural heritage.The Sundarban Reserve Forest is situated in Bangladesh and is the largest mangrove forest.
