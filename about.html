About


Eco-tourism to protect the protected mangrove forests of the Sundarbans.




The Significance of Sundarbans

The Sundarbans, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is the largest contiguous mangrove forest in the world, spanning parts of Bangladesh and India. It's a critical ecosystem, acting as a natural barrier against cyclones and tidal surges, protecting millions of people in the coastal areas.

It is globally renowned for being the home of the Royal Bengal Tiger, but its biodiversity extends far beyond, including various species of deer, crocodiles, dolphins, and a rich array of birdlife. Its unique amphibious environment supports a delicate balance of flora and fauna found nowhere else on Earth.

Our commitment to eco-tourism aims to raise awareness about this precious natural resource and contribute to its ongoing protection and research.
